Welcome

Bulletin Maker creates the five Sunday documents for you automatically. Instead of copying readings, prayers, and hymns by hand each week, you fill out a short form in your web browser and the program builds the PDFs.

You don't need to know anything technical to use it. If you can fill in a form and click a button, you can make a bulletin.

Tip: You can keep this help page open in another tab so you can read along as you go.

Your account

Everyone who makes bulletins has their own simple account — an email address and a password you choose. You never need the church's Sundays & Seasons password; a church admin links that once, behind the scenes.

  • Joining your church: ask your church admin for the invite code, click "Join with an invite code" on the sign-in screen, and pick your own email and password.
  • Signing in: your email and the password you chose. If you see "Session expired," just sign in again — nothing is lost.
  • Forgot your password? Ask your church admin or the person who runs the server for help.

Quick Start

Here's the short version, start to finish:

  1. Open Bulletin Maker in your browser and sign in.
  2. Pick the date. Click the date box at the top of Step 1 and choose the Sunday you're making the bulletin for, then click [Fetch Content].
  3. Type in the hymn numbers. Enter each hymn number and click [Fetch All Hymns]. The program looks up the titles and lyrics automatically.
  4. Click [Next] through the rest of the steps. The program fills in good defaults for the season. You only need to change something if your service is unusual.
  5. Click [Generate Selected Documents]. Wait a minute or two. Each PDF downloads in your browser — click the file names, or [Download All (.zip)] for the whole set.

Step 1 — Date & Music

This is the longest screen, but most of it is just typing in hymn numbers.

Date & Season

Click the date box and pick the Sunday, then click [Fetch Content]. The program fetches the readings and figures out the season (Advent, Lent, Easter, and so on) — you'll see a colored bar appear at the top of the window showing the season.

If the program notices anything odd about the week's content (a missing reading, for example) a yellow note will appear here — read it before you continue.

Hymns

For each hymn (Gathering, Sermon, Communion, Sending):

  1. Pick ELW (the cranberry hymnal) or ACS (the newer All Creation Sings supplement) from the small dropdown.
  2. Type the hymn number in the box next to it.
  3. Click [Fetch] — or fill in all four numbers and click [Fetch All Hymns] once.

When a hymn has more than one verse, checkboxes appear so you can untick any verses you don't want printed.

Service Music

These are the instrumental and choral pieces — prelude, offertory, postlude, and any anthem. Type the title, composer, and performer if you have them. These are optional; leave a box blank if there's nothing that week.

Step 2 — Service Settings

Everything here is pre-filled with the right defaults for the season — most weeks you can just click [Next].

  • Creed — Apostles' or Nicene
  • Kyrie / Canticle — the sung liturgy pages
  • Eucharistic Prayer — short or extended
  • Review Liturgical Texts — your church's standard texts are used automatically. Open a panel to switch to this week's Sundays & Seasons version or make a one-week edit.
  • Special Rites — tick Holy Baptism when there's a baptism; it replaces the Creed in the service.

Step 3 — Review & Generate

The review screen shows the whole service in order — every hymn, reading, and prayer, plus what's not set. Give it one look before generating.

Choose a cover image if you have one (the Luther rose or a seasonal picture), tick the documents you want, and click [Generate Selected Documents].

When it's finished you'll see a list of the files it made. Click any file name to download it, or [Download All (.zip)] for the whole set. They download like any other file — look in your browser's Downloads.

Church Settings (admins)

Click Settings in the top bar. Church admins can change:

  • What prints on the bulletins — church name, address, service time, welcome message, and the copyright footer.
  • Liturgical Setting — which ELW musical setting's sheet music appears (Setting One through Five).
  • Paper size — Legal booklet, Letter booklet, or A4.
  • The Sundays & Seasons account — linked once here; volunteers never need it.
  • The invite code — share it with volunteers so they can create their own accounts.

Reusing past bulletins

Every bulletin you generate is saved under Past Runs on Step 1. Click one to reload all its choices — hymns, music, settings — then change the date and whatever's different this week. It's the fastest way to make a typical Sunday.

If something goes wrong

"Session expired. Please sign in again."

Sign in again — this happens after several hours of inactivity and nothing is lost except what you were typing.

"No Sundays & Seasons account is linked yet"

A church admin needs to open Settings and link the church's S&S account once. If you're not an admin, ask the person who set up Bulletin Maker for your church.

A hymn won't fetch

Double-check the number and that the right hymnal (ELW or ACS) is selected. A few hymns have no printable lyrics — the program will say "title only" and still print the title.

"Where did the files go?"

They download through your browser like any other file — check your Downloads folder, or click the file names on the final screen to download them again.

The bulletin is missing something

Check the review screen on Step 3 — anything marked (not set) was left blank on Steps 1–2. Fill it in and click [Generate Selected Documents] again; regenerating is always safe.
