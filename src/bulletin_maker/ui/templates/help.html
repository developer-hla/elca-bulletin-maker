Welcome

Bulletin Maker creates the five Sunday documents for you automatically. Instead of copying readings, prayers, and hymns by hand each week, you fill out a short form and the program builds the PDFs.

You don't need to know anything technical to use it. If you can fill in a form and click a button, you can make a bulletin.

Tip: You can keep this help window open next to Bulletin Maker so you can read along as you go.

What you'll need

  • Your Sundays & Seasons account. Bulletin Maker logs in to sundaysandseasons.com on your behalf to pull readings, prayers, and hymn lyrics. Use the same username and password you use on that website.
  • The hymn numbers for the service. Usually four hymns: gathering, sermon, communion, and sending. Most come from the cranberry-colored ELW hymnal; a few may come from the ACS book.
  • An internet connection. The program has to talk to Sundays & Seasons to fetch the readings and hymn words.
  • A few minutes. Generating all five documents usually takes one to two minutes after you click [Generate].

Quick Start

Here's the short version, start to finish:

  1. Open Bulletin Maker. Double-click the program icon on your desktop.
  2. Sign in. Type your Sundays & Seasons username and password, then click [Sign In]. The program will remember you next time.
  3. Pick the date. Click in the date box at the top of Step 1 and choose the Sunday you're making the bulletin for.
  4. Type in the hymn numbers. Enter each hymn number. The program will look up the title automatically and show it back to you.
  5. Click [Next] through the rest of the steps. The program fills in good defaults for the season. You only need to change something if your service is unusual.
  6. Click [Generate Documents]. Wait a minute or two. When it's done, click [Open Folder] to see your five PDFs.

That's it. The rest of this guide walks through each screen in detail in case you ever get stuck.

Step 1 — Date & Music

This is the longest screen, but most of it is just typing in hymn numbers.

Date & Season

Click the date box and pick the Sunday you're making the bulletin for. The program will fetch the readings and figure out the season (Advent, Lent, Easter, and so on). You'll see a colored bar appear at the top of the window showing the season — that's how you know it worked.

If you pick a date and nothing happens for a moment, that's normal. The program is talking to Sundays & Seasons.

Hymns

For each hymn (Gathering, Sermon, Communion, Sending):

  1. Pick ELW or ACS from the small dropdown — ELW is the burgundy hymnal, ACS is the newer paperback supplement.
  2. Type the hymn number in the box next to it.
  3. Wait a second. The hymn title will appear once the program finds it. If it doesn't, double-check the number.

Some hymns have many verses. If you only want certain verses, click the [Choose Verses] link that appears under the hymn title and check the ones you want.

Service Music

These are the instrumental and choral pieces — prelude, offertory, postlude, and any anthem. Type the title, composer, and performer if you have them. These are optional. Leave a box blank if you don't have that information yet.

Step 2 — Service Settings

This screen controls the parts of the liturgy that change with the season — the Kyrie, the canticle, the creed, and so on. The program already filled these in for you based on the season. You usually don't need to change anything.

If the pastor asks for something different (for example, the Nicene Creed instead of the Apostles' Creed), use the dropdowns to switch it. Otherwise, click [Next].

Liturgical Settings

The choices on this part of the screen are:

  • Creed — Apostles' or Nicene
  • Kyrie — included or not
  • Canticle of Praise — Glory to God, This Is the Feast, or none
  • Eucharistic Prayer — short, poetic, or extended
  • Memorial Acclamation — included or not
  • Preface — the seasonal preface the pastor sings

If you're not sure what something is, just leave the default. The program picks the right one for the season.

Review Liturgical Texts

Lower on the screen you'll see boxes for the Confession, Offering Prayer, Prayer After Communion, Blessing, and Dismissal. Each one shows the text that will appear in the bulletin. You can:

  • Use this week's text from Sundays & Seasons (the default for most weeks)
  • Use the standard text we always use at Ascension (for example, the Aaronic Blessing for the Blessing)

Click the option you want. You can also edit the text directly if the pastor wants a small wording change.

Step 3 — Extras

This screen is for anything special this week. Most weeks you can scroll through and click [Next] without changing anything.

Cover image

If you want a picture on the front of the bulletin, click [Choose Cover Image] and pick a photo or graphic from your computer. If you leave it blank, the cover will use a plain text title.

Pictures should be JPEG or PNG. Tall pictures work better than wide ones because the cover is a portrait shape.

Baptism

Check the [Include baptism liturgy] box only if there's a baptism that Sunday. Type the names of the people being baptized in the box. The program will add the baptism liturgy in the right place in the bulletin.

Step 4 — Review & Generate

This is the final screen. You'll see a summary of everything you entered, and then the buttons that actually make the PDFs.

Service Order Review

Scroll through this part to make sure everything looks right. If something is wrong, click [Back] to fix it.

Output Settings

The output folder is where the PDFs will be saved. Click [Choose Folder] and pick a place on your computer. A good choice is a folder called "Bulletins" in your Documents. The program will remember the folder for next time.

Below the folder you'll see a list of which documents to generate. By default all five are checked. Leave them all on unless you only need one of them.

Generate Documents

Click the big [Generate Documents] button. You'll see a progress bar showing what's happening. The program is:

  1. Building the bulletin booklet
  2. Building the pulpit prayers page
  3. Building the pulpit scripture page
  4. Building the large print bulletin
  5. Building the leader guide

When it's finished, you'll see a list of the files it made and an [Open Folder] button. Click that to see your bulletins in a window you can drag, copy, or print from.

The five documents

Bulletin Maker produces five PDFs each week. Here's what each one is for:

Document Who it's for Notes
Bulletin for Congregation Everyone in the pews Print on legal-size paper, folded into a booklet (saddle-stitched).
Full with Hymns LARGE PRINT Anyone who has trouble reading small print Letter size, single column, every hymn typed out word by word.
Pulpit SCRIPTURE The lay reader Letter size, just the readings and the psalm.
Pulpit PRAYERS The prayer leader Letter size, the creed and the prayers of intercession.
Leader Guide The pastor Same as the large print, plus the music for the parts the pastor sings.

The file names all end in the date and the day name, so you can find them easily. For example: Bulletin for Congregation - 2026.04.05 - Fifth Sunday in Lent Year A.pdf

Reusing past bulletins

Every time you generate a bulletin, the program remembers what you entered. If you want to make a similar bulletin again (for example, the same date next year), you don't have to type everything in again.

On the first screen, look for the "Recent Bulletins" list. Click one to fill in all the same answers automatically. Then change the date and any hymns that are different.

To start fresh instead, click the [New Bulletin] link in the top right corner of the window.

If something goes wrong

"Sign in failed"

Almost always this means the username or password has a typo. Check carefully, watching for capital letters and the difference between the letter O and the number 0. If you're sure it's right, try signing in to sundaysandseasons.com in a web browser first to make sure your account is still active.

"Hymn not found" or the title doesn't appear

Make sure the dropdown next to the hymn number says ELW or ACS (whichever book the hymn comes from). Then double-check the number. If you typed it correctly, Sundays & Seasons may have a temporary problem — wait a minute and try again.

The program seems frozen

The first time you generate a bulletin after opening the program can be slow (twenty seconds or so) while it warms up. After that, look at the progress bar at the bottom — if it's still moving, the program is working. If it's been more than three minutes with no movement, close the window and try again.

"Generation failed" for one of the documents

If four out of five documents finish but one fails, the most common cause is a hymn lookup that didn't work. Click [Back] to Step 1, fix the hymn, and click [Generate Documents] again. The program won't re-do the documents you already have.

"Where did the files go?"

Click [Open Folder] on the final screen and a window will pop up showing all five PDFs. The folder is whatever you picked under "Output Settings" — usually a Bulletins folder in your Documents.

The bulletin is missing something

If a section of the bulletin looks wrong (for example, a hymn with the wrong verses, or the wrong creed), open Bulletin Maker again, click [Recent Bulletins] and pick the one you just made. Fix the setting that was wrong and click [Generate Documents] to make a new copy.

Updates

When a new version of Bulletin Maker is ready, you'll see a yellow bar at the top of the window that says "A new version is available."

  • Click [Install Update] to install it. The program will download the new version, replace itself, and restart automatically. This usually takes about a minute.
  • Click [What's New] to see what changed in the new version.
  • Click the small × on the right to dismiss the bar if you'd rather update later. It will come back next time you open the program.

You don't have to install every update right away, but new versions often fix bugs, so it's a good idea to install updates within a week or two.

Need more help?

If something goes wrong that this guide doesn't cover, write down exactly what you did and what the program said, then ask whoever installed Bulletin Maker for you. The more specific you can be ("I clicked Generate and got an error that said X"), the easier it is to figure out what happened.

You can also report problems on the project's GitHub page if you're comfortable doing that.
